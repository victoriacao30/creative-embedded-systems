# Sonder

This project explores the concept of sonder through a generative visualization running on an ESP32 TTGO T-Display. The installation uses generative art to reflect the simultaneous lives unfolding around us.

## Reflection 
For this ESP32 installation, I created a generative display inspired by the concept of sonder — the realization that every passerby is living a life as vivid and complex as your own.

When beginning the project, I considered what kind of artwork could make an impact on such a small screen. I encountered several challenges early on, including incorrectly calculated building dimensions, text that disappeared too quickly, and transitions that felt visually disruptive. Through debugging and recalculating layouts, I refined the composition into a clearer visual system.

Initially, I planned to display both the buildings and text on the same screen. However, repeated attempts made the display feel overcrowded. I ultimately separated them into two moments: viewers first encounter the animated cityscape on its own, allowing space for personal interpretation, and the text appears afterward to contextualize the piece. This sequencing created a stronger emotional impact than presenting everything simultaneously.

I chose the theme of sonder specifically because of the installation environment. The work was displayed alongside many classmates’ ESP32 projects, transforming the hallway into a collection of simultaneous visual experiences. Just as sonder reflects awareness of countless individual lives unfolding at once, the installation itself became a physical representation of many creative perspectives existing together.

The project reflects the idea that while we often become absorbed in our own experiences, we coexist within a much larger collective. Recognizing that others carry their own struggles and stories can shift our perspective from isolation toward shared experience. Through this piece, I aimed to encourage viewers to briefly step outside their own viewpoint and recognize the interconnected nature of the world around them.
